### Email 2: Build Your Playbook (Day 2)

**Subject:** 3 tips to build your playbook faster
**Preview text:** Route templates, AI generation, and play mirroring save serious time.

---

Coach,

You have created your first play. Here are three ways to build your playbook faster.

**1. Use route templates**
Instead of drawing every route by hand, press `R` to open the route picker. Choose from 40+ templates across short, medium, deep, screen, and blocking categories. One click applies the route to the selected player.

**2. Try AI play generation**
On the Elite plan, press `A` to open the AI generator. Type something like "Trips right, slant-flat combo with a deep post from the backside." Claude AI draws the complete play -- formation, player positions, and routes. Edit the result to fine-tune.

**3. Mirror plays**
Press `H` to flip any play horizontally. Instant left/right variation without redrawing. PlayForge creates a separate copy so you keep both versions.

**Bonus:** Use keyboard shortcuts to speed up everything. `D` for draw mode, `V` for select, `F` for formations, `L` for the play library, `Cmd+S` to save.

**[Open the Designer]**

-- The PlayForge Team

### Email 4: Build a Game Plan and Quiz (Day 10)

**Subject:** Are your players learning? Build a quiz to find out.
**Preview text:** Quizzes feed into spaced repetition -- players who struggle get more review.

---

Coach,

Your players have had access to the playbook for a few days. Time to test their understanding.

**Create a quiz:**

1. Go to **Quizzes** and click "Create Quiz"
2. Link it to your active game plan (so questions pull from the right plays)
3. Add questions -- you have 5 types to choose from:
   - **Multiple choice** -- standard knowledge check
   - **Tap-field** -- "Where does the Z receiver run this route?" (players tap a zone)
   - **Route identification** -- "What type of route is shown?"
   - **Situation match** -- "What play do you call on 3rd and short?"
   - **Assignment recall** -- "What is your assignment on HB Dive?"

4. Set a due date and share with your team

**Why it matters:**
Quiz scores feed directly into the spaced repetition system. A player who scores 40% on a play will see it scheduled for review tomorrow. A player who scores 95% will not see it again for a week. The system adapts automatically.

**[Create a Quiz]**

-- The PlayForge Team

### Email 5: Check Your Analytics (Day 21)

**Subject:** Week 3 check-in -- are your players prepared?
**Preview text:** Your analytics dashboard shows who studied and who did not.

---

Coach,

Your team has been on PlayForge for three weeks. Open your analytics dashboard and see what you find.

**What to look for:**

- **Install completion** -- What percentage of players have viewed every play in the active game plan? If it is below 80%, some players are falling behind.
- **Quiz averages** -- Are players scoring above 70%? Low averages may mean the plays need more practice time, not just more quizzes.
- **Mastery heatmap** -- The player-by-play grid shows who has mastered which plays. Look for red spots (new or learning) in plays you are running this week.
- **Inactive players** -- PlayForge flags players who have not logged in for 3+ days. A quick message to those players can make a difference.
- **Leaderboard** -- The composite score weights mastery (40%), quiz average (30%), study time (20%), and streak (10%). Share it with the team to drive healthy competition.

**One action to take today:** Find the play with the lowest install completion. Mention it at practice. Then check the dashboard tomorrow.

**[Open Analytics]**

-- The PlayForge Team
