## Slide 2: The Problem

### Your Playbook Is Stuck in 1995

Coaches spend hours creating plays on paper or in clunky legacy tools. Players receive static PDFs that sit unread in group chats.

**Pain points:**

- **Static PDFs** -- players skim once and forget
- **Clunky legacy tools** -- designed for desktop, not the mobile-first generation
- **Zero accountability** -- coaches have no idea who actually studied the playbook
- **No learning science** -- repetition and quizzing are afterthoughts, not built in
- **Format fragmentation** -- separate tools for plays, game plans, practice plans, and quizzes
- **No collaboration** -- sharing playbooks between coaches requires email chains and screenshots

> **Result:** Players show up on game day unprepared. Coaches blame effort. The real problem is the tool.

## Slide 4: Demo Highlights

### What PlayForge Looks Like

**Play Designer** -- A full-width canvas with a realistic football field. Coaches drag players, draw routes with click-to-place waypoints, pick from 24+ formations, and watch plays animate with ball flight and QB read progressions. An AI panel lets coaches type "Shotgun trips right, post-corner to the X receiver" and see it drawn instantly.

**Coach Dashboard** -- Stat cards showing install completion percentage, team quiz average, and inactive player alerts. A mastery heatmap visualizes which plays each player has mastered. A leaderboard ranks players by a composite score.

**Player View** -- A play card grid filtered by the active game plan, showing mastery badges (New, Learning, Reviewing, Mastered) on each play. A review queue surfaces plays due for spaced repetition review. Interactive quizzes test route identification, assignment recall, and situational awareness.

**Game Plan Builder** -- An ordered play list with drag-to-reorder, situation tags, and opponent/week metadata. One click makes a game plan active, instantly updating what players see.

## Slide 6: How It Works -- Coaches

### Create. Animate. Share. Track.

**Step 1: Create**
Open the play designer. Choose from 24+ formations (offense and defense) or start blank. Draw routes with click-to-place waypoints. Use 40+ route templates. Or just describe the play to the AI and let it draw.

**Step 2: Animate**
Hit play. Watch your players run routes with smooth interpolation. See the ball flight arc from QB to receiver. Visualize read progressions (1st read, 2nd read, 3rd read).

**Step 3: Share**
Build a game plan with your selected plays in order. Set it as active. Your players instantly see the plays they need to learn. Scan a QR code to onboard new players in seconds.

**Step 4: Track**
Open the analytics dashboard. See which players have viewed every play, who scored well on quizzes, who has not logged in for 3 days. Know who is prepared before game day.

---

## Slide 7: How It Works -- Players

### Learn. Quiz. Master. Compete.

**Step 1: Learn**
Open PlayForge on your phone. See the plays your coach assigned for this week. Watch each play animate. View your specific assignment per play.

**Step 2: Quiz**
Take quizzes your coach created. Identify routes on the field. Recall your assignment. Match situations to the right play call.

**Step 3: Master**
The SM-2 spaced repetition algorithm tracks your mastery. Plays start as "New" and progress through "Learning" and "Reviewing" to "Mastered." Plays you struggle with come back more often.

**Step 4: Compete**
Earn XP for views, quizzes, and mastery milestones. Unlock badges. Climb the leaderboard. See how you stack up against teammates by position group.

## Slide 8: Technology Edge

### Built Different

**AI Play Generation**
Powered by Anthropic's Claude. The system prompt encodes every formation, route template, and coordinate rule. Natural language in, complete play diagram out.

**Spaced Repetition (SM-2)**
The same algorithm used in medical school flashcard apps, applied to football plays. Scientifically proven to improve long-term retention by scheduling reviews at optimal intervals.

**Real-Time Analytics**
Not just "who logged in." PlayForge tracks per-play install completion, quiz scores per play, mastery decay over time, and study streaks. Coaches see a heatmap of their entire roster's readiness.

**Modern Stack**
Next.js 16, React 19, Konva.js canvas engine, PostgreSQL, Prisma ORM, PWA-ready. Fast server rendering, smooth client interactions, and offline-capable.

- Near-zero marginal cost per user (PostgreSQL + server-rendered app)

- High retention: coaches invest significant time building playbooks (switching cost)
